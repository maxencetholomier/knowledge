Plain text is currently the best way to store your knowledge:

- **No dependency on a company**. OneNote has great features but uses proprietary files. If Microsoft kills the app, you will need to migrate (painfully).
- **No dependency on technology**. Apple Notes makes your notes available on all your devices, but what if you don't have enough money one day to buy an iPhone?

Above all, Markdown is the best plain text format:

- **No dependency on tools**. Org-mode is fantastic but locks you to Emacs to use its full potential. This is probably why it hasn't become popular.
- **Easily readable without rendering**, unlike PDFs (LaTeX or Typst)
